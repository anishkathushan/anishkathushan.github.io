WS Ray

TELEOPERATED UNDERWATER DRONE FOR IMMERSIVE REEF INSPECTION

Project Overview

I co-designed a bio-inspired ROV for delicate, shallow-reef surveys around Hikkaduwa. The hull takes cues from the whale shark’s streamlined form to keep drag low and flow stable near sensitive corals. We iterated the shape with CFD to calm the wake while preserving agility, then verified sealing and stability with pool tests and leak checks. The vehicle uses a modular ABS outer hull with a sealed polycarbonate canister for electronics, so field repairs and swaps are quick between dives.

Control and maneuverability come from a six-thruster, 6-DoF layout tuned for fine station-keeping in surge, sway, and yaw close to the reef. The software stack runs ROS on the topside and micro-ROS on embedded controllers onboard for deterministic, low-latency control. On hardware, PID loops hold attitude and position; in simulation, a fuzzy-adaptive sliding-mode controller (FA-SMC) explores robustness against currents and disturbances. Operators get real-time video and telemetry on a desktop HMI or an immersive VR view driven by a custom handheld controller making tasks like lateral passes and hold-positions more precisely feeling natural and low-stress.

Highlights

• The hull is inspired by the whale shark and reduces drag.
• The hull fabrication is non-toxic with recyclable ABS.
• The center of gravity is adjustable with a sliding mass.
• The vehicle uses six thrusters and provides full 6-DoF control.
• The camera sits on a smooth tilt gimbal for framing.
• FA-SMC is used in simulation to test disturbance robustness.
• Video and telemetry stream in real time with low latency.
• Operators can use a desktop HMI or an immersive VR view.
• A custom hand controller maps motions to the vehicle’s body axes.
